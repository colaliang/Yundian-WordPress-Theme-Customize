Product Page Architecture Ideas

Based on your reference to Wellmax's Disc Downlight and Astra's structures, which direction fits your vision best?

A

Option A: The "Landing Page" Experience

Like the Wellmax reference. The product page acts as a full landing page. It abandons the traditional WooCommerce "Left Gallery / Right Summary" structure entirely in favor of stacked, full-width sections (Hero banner -> Key Features -> Applications -> Specs -> Inquiry).

Best for: Flagship products, single series showcase.
Trade-off: Harder to manage via standard WooCommerce product fields; usually requires an ACF flexible content layout or a page builder.
B

Option B: The "Advanced B2B Catalog" (Astra-style)

Retains the WooCommerce layout but heavily polishes it. Left side features a sticky, large image gallery (or stacked images). Right side has sticky product details, specs, and a prominent inquiry button. Below the fold are detailed tabs (Downloads, Applications, Tech Specs).

Best for: Catalogs with many products, maintaining standard WooCommerce data entry.
Trade-off: Less "custom landing page" feel, but highly consistent and easy to maintain.
C

Option C: Hybrid Approach

Top section uses the standard WooCommerce layout (Gallery + Summary) so it's easy to browse. But the bottom section (Tabs) is replaced with full-width, visually rich ACF-driven sections (e.g., Application Scenarios, Video, Big Diagrams).

Best for: Combining ease of management with visual impact.
